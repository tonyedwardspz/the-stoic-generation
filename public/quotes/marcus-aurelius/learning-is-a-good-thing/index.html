Marcus Aurelius

Learning is a good thing.

“Learning is a good thing, even for one who is growing old. From Sextus the philosopher I shall learn what I do not yet know.”

Learning is a continuous process. A never-ending journey towards wisdom and knowledge that one travels along for the course of their life. It is a process that does not discriminate based on age or social status. No matter how old one may be, there is always room for growth, for improvement and for enlightenment.

My studies of the works of Sextus, among other philosophers, serve as a testament to my belief in the importance of continuous learning. Through the teachings of these great thinkers, I have been able to expand my understanding of the world and gain new perspectives that have allowed me to lead a more fulfilling life.

In Sextus’s works, I have encountered concepts and ideas that challenge my existing beliefs, leading me to question my own assumptions and to reassess my approach to life. This process of self-reflection and introspection has allowed me to gain a deeper understanding of myself and of the world around me.

It is my belief that knowledge is power, and that power can be used for the betterment of society. The Stoic philosophy, with its emphasis on mindfulness, virtue, and wisdom, provides practical guidance on how to live a virtuous life, how to be content in difficult circumstances and how to approach the challenges of life with equanimity.

In modern times, the Stoic philosophy remains relevant, its teachings providing a road map for individuals seeking to live a more fulfilling life. By embracing Stoicism, one can cultivate inner peace, develop a deeper understanding of the world, and lead a life characterized by wisdom, compassion, and fortitude.

In conclusion, learning is a valuable tool for self-improvement and the pursuit of wisdom, regardless of one’s age. The Stoic philosophy, with its emphasis on mindfulness, virtue, and wisdom, provides practical guidance on how to live a virtuous life and approach the challenges of life with equanimity. By embracing its teachings, one can lead a more fulfilling life, characterized by wisdom, compassion, and fortitude.
